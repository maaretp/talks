# Exploratory Testing Reimagined - Task Expansion in Talent

_Length: 45 min_

One type of coverage matters more than others in testing: results. Empirical, actionable, up-to-date information, written in invisible ink for testing to reveal. Instead of asking for test cases, could we just ask for the information? And instead of reporting the information as bug reports, could we capture the problems in automation, delivered on side of the fix?

In this talk, we reimagine exploratory testing as I have experienced it:

- Information intake shapes what we fail to see, and we can move beyond the failure.
- Test cases are primarily captured programmatically as test automation, as output of exploring.
- There are no manual test cases with steps, lists of ideas of explore,
- Testing does not end with report and retesting, but expands to debugging and repairs.
- AI raises the bar of what is possible.

We challenge three habits we need to outgrow. First, asking for test cases. Second, defaulting value to Jira tickets. Third, settling for information that is not actionable.

I'll demo an application with problems. We'll walk through the shift together: from test cases to a problem list, from a problem list to test automation that captures each issue, and from there to delivered fixes. This is task expansion — the kind that the age of AI not only enables but expects from us. When generating a test and a fix becomes fast and cheap, the bottleneck moves to the one thing that hasn't been automated: the exploring mind deciding where to look next, and recognizing what the invisible ink reveals.

The age of AI brings forth a significant opportunity of task expansion natural to exploratory testing. Instead of exploring for bugs in the traditional sense, we explore for intent, value, context, alignment, and experience information. We share to raise the bar for everyone - it's about testing more than testers.
